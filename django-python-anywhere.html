Deploying a Django App to Python Anywhere

Posted by Bob on Sat 22 July 2017 in Django • 1 min read

After Julian's great article on deploying a Flask app to Heroku, let's look at another provider: PythonAnywhere. And let's deploy a Django app.

In this article I will deploy our PyPlanet Article Sharer App to PythonAnywhere.

Pricing

Before getting starting check out pricing. I was going with the free version, but as we scrape Planet Python which is not on their whitelist I had to go with the "Hacker tier". This also opens SSH access, custom domain name and an extra web worker. The real nice benefit of the first paid tier is 20 scheduled tasks hourly or daily vs just 1 daily task which is pretty limited. Adding a postgres DB will cost 7 dollar more so I might want to look at Heroku too.

Project setup

As the Hacker tier just gives me one app I made this container project / repo to host multiple apps in. It is in lign with Django's elegant architectura: one master project with one or more apps which are easy to add or remove.
